# ADR-002

## Title

Adoption of a Documentation-First Information Architecture

---

## Status

Accepted

---

## Context

Many websites evolve by adding pages whenever a new idea appears.

This often leads to inconsistent navigation, duplicated content, and poor discoverability.

---

## Decision

FYNLY adopts a documentation-first Information Architecture.

Every page must have:

- A single purpose
- A defined audience
- A clear relationship to the rest of the ecosystem

No page may be added without fitting into the documented architecture.

---

## Consequences

Positive

- Consistent navigation
- Better scalability
- Easier maintenance
- Improved user experience

Trade-offs

- Requires planning before implementation
- New pages must be evaluated before addition

---

## Conclusion

The Information Architecture becomes the blueprint for every future page and feature within the FYNLY ecosystem.
